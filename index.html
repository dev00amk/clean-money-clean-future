What is "Dirty Money"?

Money obtained through illegal means including drug trafficking, corruption, tax evasion, fraud, or other criminal activities. Understanding the source of your partner's wealth is crucial for protecting yourself legally and financially.

Money Laundering

The process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate through complex transfers and transactions. Be aware of unexplained wealth that doesn't match documented income sources.

Source of Funds

Documentation proving where money or assets originated, including employment, business income, investments, gifts, or inheritance. Legitimate wealth should have clear, verifiable documentation.

Politically Exposed Persons (PEP)

Individuals with prominent public positions who may have increased corruption risk. This includes government officials, their family members, and close associates who may face enhanced scrutiny.

Financial Abuse Warning Signs

  • Controlling access to bank accounts or financial information
  • Preventing you from working or accessing your own money
  • Hiding financial documents or being secretive about money
  • Pressuring you to sign financial documents without explanation
  • Stealing your identity or using your credit without permission

Know Your Customer (KYC)

Processes used by financial institutions to verify identity and assess money laundering risks. Understanding these processes helps you recognize legitimate versus suspicious financial behavior.

Common Red Flags

  • Lifestyle that doesn't match declared income
  • Reluctance to provide financial documentation
  • Cash-heavy business with unclear records
  • Frequent unexplained large transactions
  • Offshore accounts without clear business purpose
  • Associates with questionable backgrounds

Protection Strategies

  • Prenuptial agreements with asset disclosure requirements
  • Separate bank accounts and credit monitoring
  • Professional background checks and verification
  • Legal consultation before major financial commitments
  • Regular financial reviews and audit rights
  • Insurance and asset protection planning

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Significant unexplained wealth or income
  • Complex business structures or offshore involvement
  • Criminal history or ongoing legal issues
  • High-value assets requiring protection
  • International or cross-border relationships
  • Signs of financial abuse or control
